Cosmetic Dentistry

Cosmetic dentistry includes dental procedures aimed at improving the appearance of teeth. In many cases, these procedures also have health benefits. For example, a crown can cover a deformed tooth while protecting it from potential damage.

Difference between Crowns and Veneers

A veneer is a thin layer of Laminate that covers the front of the tooth, usually for aesthetic reasons. A crown, on the other hand, covers the entire surface of the tooth, including the back and sides.

Laminate Veneers

Veneers composed of Laminate are very thin coverings that are created to suit each tooth precisely. They are utilized to enhance the teeth's size, position, alignment, color, form, and general appearance. The ideal option to improve and transform your smile veneers.

Steps to Your New Veneer Smile

- First of all, during the first appointment after the consultation, our specialist preps your teeth then takes impressions for the lab.
- After finishing the impressions, we will place them temporarily to limit the sensitivity. In the meantime, all of the molds and images will be sent to the lab where veneers will be customized.
- Days later, we will remove your temporaries and fit each of the veneers with the appropriate teeth, which is permanently bonded.

How Long Porcelain Veneers Last

Laminate veneers last up to 15-20 years, but it depends on how well you maintain them. Regular maintenance is important to their longevity so you can get the most out of them. Porcelain veneers are a great way to improve your smile. In some cases, they can provide you with lasting benefits without the need for orthodontic treatment.

Dental Bonding

Dentists may recommend dental bonding for a number of dental conditions. These include cracked or chipped teeth, decayed teeth, gaps between teeth, discolored teeth, short teeth, receding gums that cause root sensitivity, poorly shaped teeth, and more. Since bonding is the least invasive dental procedure, it is recommended that patients with missing or broken teeth have it prior to veneers. The bonding process usually does not shave the tooth and, if at all, requires a slight roughening of the tooth structure that is nearly reversible and not even visible to the naked eye.

Zirconium Crown

Dental crowns have many benefits, such as strengthening tooth structure, improving tooth function, and serving as a roof or cover for teeth after root canal treatment. Zirconia dental caps are known to deliver great results - they make your teeth and smile more attractive. Zirconia dental caps have many advantages. They are metal-free and more durable. Their high strength and optimal esthetics compared to traditional metal-ceramic crowns make them the first choice among all dental crowns. Zirconia crowns are undoubtedly the most popular choice.

Dental Bridge

A bridge is a permanent replacement for one or more missing teeth that can be attached to existing teeth or implants in the gums. Dental bridges are usually made of porcelain or zirconia or a mixture of porcelain and other materials to create realistic bridges and natural smiles. There are four different types of bridges you can choose from, depending on where the bridge is in your mouth
